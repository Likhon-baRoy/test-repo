* The Mental Model: Think Aloud
In a CP contest, only the *result* matters. In an interview, the *process* is the product.
- *Goal:* Make your brain "transparent" to the interviewer.
- *Rule:* If you aren't typing, you should be talking.



* The Timeline Strategy

** T-Minus 1 Month: The Breadth Phase
- Focus on *Patterns*, not problems.
- Ensure your =data_structures/= and =algorithms/= folders are 80% complete.
- Solve 2-3 "Medium" problems daily across different topics.

** T-Minus 1 Week: The Depth Phase
- Re-solve your *Mistakes Log* in =revision/mistakes.org=.
- Practice "Blind Coding" (no IDE/LSP assistance).
- Review time and space complexities for everything in your =README.org=.

** T-Minus 1 Day: The Preservation Phase
- Stop solving new problems.
- Read through your "Intuition" sections in your Org files.
- Ensure your Emacs environment (or the interview platform) is ready.

* The Problem-Solving Framework (5 Steps)
Use this exact order during the interview:

1. *Clarify:* Ask about edge cases. "Can the array be empty?" "Are there negative numbers?"
2. *Brute Force:* State the $O(N^2)$ or $O(2^N)$ approach first. *Never* start coding yet.
3. *Optimize:* Look for bottlenecks. Can I use a *Hash Table* to get $O(1)$ lookup? Can I use *Two Pointers*?
4. *Dry Run:* Walk through your logic with a small test case *before* writing a line of code.
5. *Code:* Write clean, modular code.

* High-Frequency Patterns Checklist
Keep track of your comfort level with these "Big Winners":

- [ ] *Sliding Window:* (Strings, contiguous subarrays)
- [ ] *Two Pointers:* (Sorted arrays, Linked List middle/cycle)
- [ ] *Fast & Slow Pointers:* (Linked List cycles)
- [ ] *Merge Intervals:* (Scheduling problems)
- [ ] *Topological Sort:* (Dependency tasks)
- [ ] *Recursion/Backtracking:* (Subsets, Permutations)

* The "Emergency" Cheat Sheet
If you get stuck, ask yourself these "Rescue Questions":
1. Can I sort the input? ($O(N \log N)$ is often acceptable).
2. Is there a pattern in the *prefix sums*?
3. Can I use a *Heap* to track the Top K elements?
4. Am I repeating work that a *Hash Table* could store?
